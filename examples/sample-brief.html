Competitor Analysis

The dental AI receptionist market is crowded with 10+ direct competitors (Arini, Dentina, Rondah, Phonely, etc.) offering similar call answering and scheduling at $50-$300/month — a fraction of human receptionist costs ($35,000+/year). Indirect competition comes from established patient communication platforms (PracticeMojo, Adit, CareStack) that automate reminders and reviews but don't handle inbound calls. Key opportunities lie in deeper PMS integration, serving independent 1-2 location clinics with simplified onboarding, and expanding beyond call answering into post-appointment patient engagement. Market data shows 30-35% of calls are missed and 67% never call back, with new patients worth $1,200-$1,500 in year one, creating strong ROI justification.

Substitutes

  • Practice management software built-in patient communication (SMS/email only) — handles reminders but doesn't answer inbound calls

  • Integration depth — most AI receptionists claim PMS integration but actual two-way sync quality varies; opportunity for deep, native integrations with top dental software (Dentrix, Eaglesoft, OpenDental) that go beyond basic appointment booking to include insurance verification, treatment plan follow-up, and recall campaigns

  • Walk me through what happened the last time your front desk was overwhelmed with calls. What did you do? What did patients experience?

  • Tell me about a specific patient you lost to another practice in the last 3 months. What was the situation?
  • What tools or systems have you tried in the past year to handle phone calls or appointment reminders? What happened? Are you still using them? If not, why did you stop?
  • When you last hired a front desk person, how long did it take to hire and train them? What did that cost you in time and money?
  • How much do you currently spend per month on phone systems, patient communication tools, and front desk staffing combined? Walk me through the breakdown.
  • If you could wave a magic wand and fix one thing about how your practice handles phone calls, what would it be? Why that specific thing?
  • Have you ever looked into AI receptionists or answering services before? If yes: what stopped you from buying? If no: have you heard of them? What do you think about them?
  • What would need to be true for you to consider replacing or supplementing your current phone system? What's your biggest concern about making that kind of change?
  • Tell me about the last time a patient left a negative review about difficulty reaching your office. What happened?

Track: (1) total after-hours calls received per practice, (2) % that are appointment-booking vs. emergencies vs. spam, (3) % you successfully book into their calendar (requires them to give you PMS access or accept manual email handoffs), (4) patient satisfaction (ask the practice to survey patients who booked via you). If practices refuse PMS access or won't do the email handoff work, that's a critical failure signal (integration is non-negotiable). Cost: $0 (your time) + $15 (Google Voice number).
