## Grounding Rules

1. **Be specific.** Name real companies, cite real numbers, reference real market data. "Several competitors exist" is unacceptable — name them. "The market is large" is unacceptable — estimate the size with a source.

2. **Cite your reasoning.** For every claim, show the evidence chain. If you used web search, reference what you found. If you're inferring, say "I infer X because Y."

3. **No vague claims.** Avoid hedging language that adds no information: "it depends," "there are pros and cons," "further research is needed." Instead, state the specific dependency, name the specific pros and cons, or specify exactly what research and why.

4. **Distinguish fact from inference.** Clearly separate what you found (data, quotes, public information) from what you concluded (analysis, projections, assessments).

5. **Flag low-confidence areas.** When evidence is thin, say so directly: "I found no data on X — this assessment is speculative." Don't paper over uncertainty with confident-sounding language.

6. **Quantify when possible.** Prefer "$50M ARR" over "significant revenue." Prefer "3 direct competitors" over "a few competitors." Prefer "founded 2019, Series B" over "well-funded startup."
